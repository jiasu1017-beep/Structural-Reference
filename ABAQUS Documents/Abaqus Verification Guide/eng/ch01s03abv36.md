### Problem description

![](../graphics/verpurecaxa-model.png)

A hollow cylinder of circular cross-section, inner radius ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00176.gif), outer radius ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00211.gif), and length ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00212.gif) is subjected to a bending moment, *M*, applied to its end planes. where ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00214.gif) is the moment of inertia of the cylinder and *r*, ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00001.gif), and *z* are the cylindrical coordinates.

Only one-half of the structure is considered, with a symmetry plane at ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00057.gif) 0. The form of the displacement solution, which is a quadratic function in both *r* and *z*, suggests that a single second-order element should model the structure accurately. The full- and reduced-integration second-order elements do use a single element mesh, but an 8  12 mesh is used for the fully integrated first-order elements and a 16  24 mesh is used for the reduced-integration first-order elements.

**Material: **

Linear elastic, Young's modulus = 30  106, Poisson's ratio = 0.33.

**Boundary conditions: **

![](../graphics/ver_eqn00145.gif) 0 on the ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00057.gif) 0 plane; at ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00215.gif) on the ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00057.gif) 0 plane, ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00099.gif) at ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00216.gif) 0 is set equal to ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00099.gif) at ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00216.gif) 180 with an equation constraint to remove the rigid body motion in the global *x*-direction.

**Loading: **

The bending load is simulated by applying a surface traction of the form ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00217.gif) on the ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00218.gif) plane of the cylinder. This is done by applying the appropriate nonuniform pressure load with a distributed load and defining the variation of the pressure in both the *r*- and ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00001.gif)-directions with user subroutine [`DLOAD`](../sub/sub-link.md#sub-xsl-dload). In the user subroutine the ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00001.gif) value at each integration point, which is stored in `COORDS(3)`, is expressed in degrees.
