# 1.5.4 Patch test for axisymmetric elements







**Products: **Abaqus/Standard  Abaqus/Explicit  

### Elements tested

CAX3    CAX3H    CAX3T    CAX4    CAX4H    CAX4I    CAX4IH    CAX4R    CAX4RH    CAX4RHT    CAX4RT    

CAX6    CAX6H    CAX6M    CAX6MH    CAX8    CAX8H    CAX8R    CAX8RH    

### Problem description

![](../graphics/veraxipatch-model.png)

**Material: **

Linear elastic, Young's modulus = 1.0  106, Poisson's ratio = 0.25.

For coupled temperature-displacement elements dummy thermal properties are prescribed to complete the material definition.

**Loading for Step 1: **

Displacement boundary conditions applied to all exterior nodes: ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00409.gif) 103*r*, ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00393.gif) 103![](../graphics/ver_eqn00410.gif).

Nonuniform body force: To maintain a constant shear stress ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00051.gif) 400 and preserve equilibrium, an equilibrating body force, BZNU, is defined in user subroutine [`DLOAD`](../sub/sub-link.md#sub-xsl-dload) as BZNU ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00411.gif) 400![](../graphics/ver_eqn00412.gif), where *r* is the radius of the integration point.

In the Abaqus/Explicit simulations this step is followed by an intermediate step in which the model is returned to its unloaded state.

**Loading for Step 2: **

Displacement boundary conditions applied to all exterior nodes: ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00409.gif) 102*r*, ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00393.gif) 102*z*.

**Loading for Step 3: **

Displacement boundary conditions applied to the deformed geometry of Step 2 at all exterior nodes: ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00409.gif) 103*r*, ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00393.gif) 103![](../graphics/ver_eqn00410.gif).

Nonuniform body force (as described for Step 1): BZNU ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00416.gif) 400![](../graphics/ver_eqn00412.gif).

In the Abaqus/Standard simulations this step is defined as a perturbation step; in the Abaqus/Explicit simulations a velocity boundary condition that gives rise to the perturbation is specified instead.

All elements yield exact solutions.
