# 52.2 Stress linearization example







[Figure 52--2](pt05ch52s02.md#usv-stresslin-exa) shows an example of a stress line defined for an axisymmetric model of a pressure vessel.  

**Figure 52–2** Stress line through an axisymmetric model of a pressure vessel.

![](../graphics/usv-stresslin-exa-nls.png)

 The stress line `Section_A_B` is defined through the vessel wall. [Figure 52--3](pt05ch52s02.md#usv-stresslin-dialog) and [Figure 52--4](pt05ch52s02.md#usv-stresslin-computation) show the basic settings and computations, respectively, that you use to linearize the S22 stress component for the undeformed model shape.  

**Figure 52–3** Stress linearization basic specifications.

![](../graphics/usv-stresslin-dialog-nls.png)

**Figure 52–4** Stress linearization computations.

![](../graphics/usv-stresslin-computation-nls.png)

 When you click **OK** or **Apply** in the **Stress Linearization** dialog box, Abaqus/CAE creates an *X–Y* plot of the S22 stress component (oriented normal to the stress line) and of the resulting linearized stresses, as shown in [Figure 52--5](pt05ch52s02.md#usv-stresslin-exaplot).  

**Figure 52–5** Linearized stress plot.

![](../graphics/usv-stresslin-exaplot.png)
