# 20.8.3 Modifying or copying objects by selecting edges







When you move edges, Abaqus/CAE repositions the selected edges and uses the **During edit** constraint solution method to modify any connected edges (for more information, see ["Customizing the use of constraints in the Sketcher," Section 20.9.10](pt03ch20s09hlb10.md)). The sketch constraints and constraint solution method may restrict the types of moves that you can make or cause Abaqus/CAE to reposition edges that you did not select. When you copy edges, the copies are positioned according to the copy method—the copied edges are not affected by any existing sketch constraints. You cannot copy objects between sketches. Abaqus/CAE provides the following methods for moving or copying selected edges:

**Translate**

You can translate selected edges by specifying the start and end coordinates of a translation vector. The translation vector can start from any point; however, you may find it more meaningful to start the vector from the vertex at one end of a selected edge and end it at the vertex's new location. [Figure 20--12](pt03ch20s08s03.md#ske-tech-trans) shows the selected edges, the translation vector, and the result of the translation. To achieve the results shown, **Fixed** constraints were added to prevent the two vertices of the upper rectangle from moving. 

**Figure 20–12** Translating selected edges.

![](../graphics/ske-tech-trans-nls.png)

**Rotate**

You can rotate selected edges by specifying the coordinates of the center of rotation and entering the angle of rotation; a positive angle indicates a counterclockwise rotation. [Figure 20--13](pt03ch20s08s03.md#ske-tech-rotate) shows the selected edges, the selected center of rotation, and the result of a 90 rotation. The selected edges are not connected or otherwise constrained to other edges in the sketch, so the constraint solution method has no effect on the rotation.

**Figure 20–13** Rotating selected edges.

![](../graphics/ske-tech-rotate-nls.png)

**Mirror**

You can mirror selected edges by selecting a straight line in the sketch as the mirror line. [Figure 20--14](pt03ch20s08s03.md#ske-tech-mirror) shows the selected edges, the mirror line, and the resulting copy including a mirror constraint for each of the four selected edges. The mirror line may be any straight object line or construction line in the sketch.

**Figure 20–14** Mirroring selected edges.

![](../graphics/ske-tech-mirror.png)

**Scale**

You can scale selected edges by specifying a center point and a scale factor. A scale factor less than 1.0 decreases the spacing between the edges, and a scale factor greater than 1.0 increases the spacing between the edges. [Figure 20--15](pt03ch20s08s03.md#ske-tech-scale) shows the selected edges, the selected center point, and the result for a scale factor of 1.5. 

**Figure 20–15** Scaling selected edges.

![](../graphics/ske-tech-scale-nls.png)
