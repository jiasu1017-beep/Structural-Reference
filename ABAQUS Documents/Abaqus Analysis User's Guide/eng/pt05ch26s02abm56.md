### Overview

A material's specific heat:
- is required for transient ["Uncoupled heat transfer analysis," Section 6.5.2](pt03ch06s05at18.md); transient ["Fully coupled thermal-stress analysis," Section 6.5.3](pt03ch06s05at19.md); transient ["Coupled thermal-electrical analysis," Section 6.7.3](pt03ch06s07at22.md); and ["Adiabatic analysis," Section 6.5.4](pt03ch06s05at20.md);
- must be defined for an Abaqus/CFD analysis when the energy equation is active (["Energy equation" in "Incompressible fluid dynamic analysis," Section 6.6.2](pt03ch06s06aus48.md#usb-anl-aifluiddyn-energy));
- must appear in conjunction with a density definition (see ["Density," Section 21.2.1](pt05ch21s02abm01.md));
- can be linear or nonlinear (by defining it as a function of temperature); and
- can be specified as a function of temperature and/or field variables.

The specific heat per unit mass is given as a function of temperature and field variables. By default, specific heat at constant volume is assumed.

### Elements

Specific heat effects can be defined for all heat transfer, coupled thermal-electrical-structural, coupled temperature-displacement, coupled thermal-electrical, and fluid elements in Abaqus. Specific heat can also be defined for stress/displacement elements for use in adiabatic stress analysis.

Specific heat must be defined for all transient thermal analyses even if the only elements in the model are user-defined elements (["User-defined elements," Section 32.15.1](pt06ch32s15alm60.md)), in which case a dummy specific heat must be specified.
