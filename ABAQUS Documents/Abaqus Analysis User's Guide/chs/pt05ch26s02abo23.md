# 26.2.1 Thermal properties: overview







The following properties describe the thermal behavior of a material and can be used in heat transfer and thermal stress analyses (see ["Heat transfer analysis procedures: overview," Section 6.5.1](pt03ch06s05abo08.md)):

**Thermal conductivity**: When heat flows by conduction, the thermal conductivity must be defined (["Conductivity," Section 26.2.2](pt05ch26s02abm55.md)).

**Specific heat**: In transient heat transfer analyses as well as adiabatic stress analyses the specific heat of a material must be defined (["Specific heat," Section 26.2.3](pt05ch26s02abm56.md)).

**Latent heat**: When a material changes phase, the change in internal energy can be significant. The amount of energy liberated or absorbed can be defined by specifying a latent heat for each phase change a material undergoes (["Latent heat," Section 26.2.4](pt05ch26s02abm57.md)).
