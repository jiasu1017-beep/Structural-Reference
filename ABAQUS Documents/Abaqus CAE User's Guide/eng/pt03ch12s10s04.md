# 12.10.4 Specifying a joule heat fraction







Joule heating arises when the energy dissipated by an electrical current flowing through a conductor is converted into thermal energy. A joule heat fraction is the fraction of dissipated electrical energy released as heat in coupled thermal-electrical problems.

See the following sections for more information:
- ["Coupled thermal-electrical analysis," Section 6.7.3 of the Abaqus Analysis User's Guide](../usb/usb-link.md#usb-anl-ajouleheating)
- ["Specifying the amount of thermal energy generated due to electrical current" in "Coupled thermal-electrical analysis," Section 6.7.3 of the Abaqus Analysis User's Guide](../usb/usb-link.md#usb-anl-ajouleheating-fraction)

**To specify a joule heat fraction:**

1. From the menu bar in the **Edit Material** dialog box, select ****Thermal**![](../graphics/images/arrow.gif)**Joule Heat Fraction****. (For information on displaying the **Edit Material** dialog box, see ["Creating or editing a material," Section 12.7.1](pt03ch12s07hlb01.md).)
2. In the **Fraction** field, enter the fraction of electrical energy released as heat, including any unit conversion factor. The default value is 1.0.
3. Click **OK** to close the **Edit Material** dialog box. Alternatively, you can select another material behavior to define from the menus in the **Edit Material** dialog box (see ["Browsing and modifying material behaviors," Section 12.7.2](pt03ch12s07hlb02.md), for more information).
