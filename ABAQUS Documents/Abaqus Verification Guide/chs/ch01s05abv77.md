# 1.5.9 Patch test for thermal-electrical elements







**Product: **Abaqus/Standard

### Problem description

The meshes used for the thermal-electrical element tests are the same as those used for the corresponding heat transfer elements.

**Boundary conditions: **

![](../graphics/ver_eqn00439.gif), where *T* is the temperature, ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00264.gif) through ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00440.gif) are arbitrary constants, and *x*, *y*, *z* denote spatial location. ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00441.gif), where ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00202.gif) is the electrical potential, ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00442.gif) through ![](../graphics/ver_eqn00443.gif) are arbitrary constants, and *x*, *y*, *z* denote spatial location. Temperature and electrical potentials are prescribed at every node along the boundary of the mesh.

### Reference solution

Fluxes: Since the temperature and electrical potential fields are chosen to be linear, they have constant spatial gradients and, thus, have constant fluxes at every integration point.

### Results and discussion

All elements yield exact solutions.
